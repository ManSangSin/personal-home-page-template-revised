About Me

Hello world, thank you for visiting my page. From a young age, I have always enjoyed playing about with technology. I have a vivid memory of my older brother unboxing his very own computer (Packard Bell) and how advanced it was with its huuuge 20GB IDE hard drive running Windows Millenium. I recall the CRT monitor which came bundled with the computer, the screensize was sooo small compared to how big and boxy the actual monitor was (unlike our current "flatscreens" 😂) but it was still pretty amazing to the young me at the time. Many many hours was wasted playing solitaire and 3D pinball whilst listening to Winamp and watching its cool visualisations. Move along ~ decade, I watched in awe as my brother built his first custom PC and a few more years after that was when I was lucky enough to build my very own first PC. Even now, I am still using it and periodically upgrading parts (to be honest, other than the case, every other component has been swapped out lol.)

Now, with my many years of experience messing about with different computers (touching on linux, raspbian, android, windows etc) and watching a lot of random tutorials online has made me the designated "tech guy" of the household. Any technical issues? No problem! Need advice purchasing tech? Ask away! I may not know the correct answer but I will try and help the best I can with what I know 😊.

So, lets see... before my journey into coding, I studied Structural & Architectural Engineering in Strathclyde University. I learned a lot from my course, I enjoyed the calulations aspect, it made me more curious as a person of how things work and it help develop a lot of my core skills including presenting, teamworking, problem solving, time management and much much more but ultimately I did not felt it was right path for me.

Currently I am working on an online course which is taught by google over on Coursera about Google IT Automation with Python. I have learned a lot of different concepts including "Python", Git" and IT automation using "Puppet". I have thoroughly enjoyed the course so far and would like to continue doing something similar which is why I decided to apply to CYF!
